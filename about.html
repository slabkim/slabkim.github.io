Hello! I'm Kim.

Before I discovered my passion for technology, I was just a curious kid with an old laptop and a lot of questions.

As a computer science student at Universitas Lampung, I’ve found joy not only in solving problems but also in understanding how those problems affect people. Whether I’m debugging a program or brainstorming ideas for a group project, I always find myself drawn to the "why" just as much as the "how". Each semester brings a new challenge that reshapes how I think.

In the teams I work with, I aim to bring clarity and connection. I want everyone to feel like their voice matters, and that no idea is too small to explore. I believe great work starts with open minds—and that learning together makes everything better.

Programming became more than just code for me when I realized its power to create impact—from automating simple tasks to building something people can rely on. The more I explored tech, the more I saw its potential to improve lives. That's when I knew I didn’t just want to code—I wanted to create.

Why software development?

Because at its core, software is a tool for empowerment. My journey has taught me that empathy and logic go hand in hand, and that thoughtful code can solve real problems. I believe the best software comes from listening deeply, staying curious, and always striving to make things better than before.
